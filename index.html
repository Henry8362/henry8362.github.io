The problem of amenable deaths

597,000 deaths occured in the UK in 2016, 75,736 of these were deemed amenable by the UK Office of National Statistics.

A large proportion of these deaths occur in hospitals.

Did you know?

35%-64% of all hospital deaths or adverse events such as spsis or acute kidney injury were not recognised early enough and these patients received sub-optimal care.

The severity in a patient's illness is missed because the traditional methods of identification are no longer able to cope with the speed, volume or complexity of data a patient generates, nor are they able to identify the subtle patterns of disease and deterioration.

Early identification and optimal treatment could prevent up to half these adverse events.
